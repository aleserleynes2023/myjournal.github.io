Date: May 15, 2025, 1:36 A.M.

Nights I Wake Missing Her

Dear Journal,

I often find myself waking up in the middle of the night somewhere between 12:00 and 2:00 a.m.and without even thinking, the first thing I do is check my phone. Just hoping… maybe there’s a message from her. But most of the time, there’s nothing. And if I’m being honest, it tears me apart more than I care to admit.

That’s when the thoughts start creeping in. All the overthinking. I just lie there, staring at the ceiling or into the darkness of my room, questioning my choices, replaying moments, wondering where I went wrong or what I should’ve done differently.

Sometimes I wish it wasn’t long-distance. I wish she lived just a few minutes away from my hometown. I wish I had the chance to court her in person bring her flowers, take her on spontaneous dates, and simply be there. Physically. Emotionally. Every day.

But life isn’t a beautiful fantasy where everything goes your way. I understand that. Maybe this is all happening for a reason God’s test, perhaps? Still, I hate how often she’s the first thing that comes to mind every day. I wonder: even when she’s busy, does she ever think of me too? Does she know how it feels to wait in silence, hoping for even just a single hello that could ease the weight I carry?

A simple message. A short conversation to ask how we’ve both been. That’s all it would take to turn this heavy feeling around.

And strangely enough, the more distant things feel between us, the deeper I fall for her. It’s confusing, overwhelming and sometimes, hard to carry. But I guess that’s the thing with real love… it doesn't vanish just because things get hard.

I don’t even know how to put all these thoughts into one clean paragraph. But I’m glad I started this journal. Somehow, it’s helping me unload the weight I can’t express anywhere else. It’s helping me heal slowly and reflect.

So here I am, again. Writing for her. Writing for myself.

—Aleser
