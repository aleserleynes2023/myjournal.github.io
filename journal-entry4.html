Date: May 14, 2025, 6:25 P.M.

A Love I Still Hold On To

Dear Journal,

I still miss her the person I truly love, Alesia.

From the moment I met her, something deep inside told me she was the one I truly wanted. Not just for a fleeting moment, but for the kind of forever that people write songs and stories about. And I knew, love isn't just about saying the words it’s about proving it through actions.

But whenever I hurt her, even unintentionally, it shattered me. It’s one of the worst pains to hurt the very person your heart beats for. Whenever we had misunderstandings, all I ever wished for was a moment to talk things through. Yet most of the time, I’m left confused, wondering what’s really going through her mind.

I ask myself the quiet questions:
Why can’t she reassure me sometimes?
Why can’t she send even just one reply?
Why does she always feel so distant, so busy?

These thoughts hurt. They make me wonder if maybe she doesn’t value me as much as I hoped she would. And on darker days, I spiral into asking if she’s already lost feelings… or worse was it just infatuation from the beginning?

But I can’t deny what I felt during the three months I loved her deeply those were some of the most joyful and hopeful days I’ve ever known. Even now, through all these low points and moments of sadness, I still choose to continue. Because I made a promise to wait for her… and I intend to keep it.

The truth is, I only fear one thing:
Is she scared to lose me too?
Or will she one day turn away like I never even existed?

Maybe she’s the one I’ll marry.
Or maybe… she’ll become my greatest heartbreak.
But I remain faithful.

Sometimes when I reflect, I realize that much of this is just fear playing tricks on my mind. You attract what you constantly fear. So now, I pray for peace. For a cleansed, quiet mind. I pray to think positive thoughts because deep down, I believe there’s a reason why I felt this love for her. It wasn’t random. It wasn’t meaningless.

And I’m not going to waste it.

—Aleser
